9709 Math / Mechanics / Q7

Given: Particle A ($6.5$ kg) on rough slope ($\tan\alpha = \frac{5}{12}$, $\mu=0.4$). Particle B ($m$ kg) hangs vertically.
(a) Find possible values of $m$ for equilibrium.
(b) If $m=12$, released from rest. Find speed after moving $0.6$m using Energy Method.
Net PE Loss KE Gain Work vs Friction
Speed: 0.5x
v = 0.00 m/s
Part (a): Equilibrium (7 marks)
Resolve forces on A:
$\sin\alpha = \frac{5}{13}$, $\cos\alpha = \frac{12}{13}$
$F_{max} = \mu R = 0.4(6.5g \cos\alpha) = 24$ N
Weight component $P = 6.5g \sin\alpha = 25$ N
Case 1: A about to slip DOWN
Friction acts UP slope.
$T + F = P \Rightarrow T = 25 - 24 = 1$ N
$T = mg \Rightarrow \mathbf{m = 0.1}$

Case 2: A about to slip UP
Friction acts DOWN slope.
$T = P + F \Rightarrow T = 25 + 24 = 49$ N
$T = mg \Rightarrow \mathbf{m = 4.9}$
Ans: $0.1 \le m \le 4.9$
Part (b) Step 1: Energy Changes
Dist $s = 0.6$, Mass $m = 12$
PE Loss of B: $12g(0.6) = \mathbf{72}$ J
PE Gain of A: $6.5g(0.6) \sin\alpha = \mathbf{15}$ J
Net PE Loss = 72 - 15 = 57 J
2. Work Done against Friction
$WD = F \times s$
$WD = 24 \times 0.6 = \mathbf{14.4}$ J
3. Conservation of Energy
$Net\ PE\ Loss = KE\ Gain + WD$
$57 = \frac{1}{2}(12+6.5)v^2 + 14.4$
$42.6 = 9.25v^2$
$v = \sqrt{\frac{42.6}{9.25}} \approx \mathbf{2.15}$ m/s
