A box of mass $25$ kg is pulled $12$ m up a rough plane inclined at an angle of $8^\circ$ to the horizontal. The box moves up a line of greatest slope against a frictional force of $50$ N. The force pulling the box is parallel to the line of greatest slope.

The box starts from rest and has speed $2.4$ ms$^{-1}$ at the end of the $12$ m.

Find the work done by the pulling force. [4]

Step 0: Extract & List Data
$m = 25 \text{ kg}, \quad s = 12 \text{ m}$
$\theta = 8^\circ, \quad F_{\text{fric}} = 50 \text{ N}$
$u = 0, \quad v = 2.4 \text{ ms}^{-1}$
1. Calculate Energy Changes (PE & KE)
The box gains Potential Energy as it rises and Kinetic Energy as it speeds up.
(i) Gain in PE ($mgh$):
Vertical height $h = s \sin\theta = 12 \sin 8^\circ$
$PE = 25 \times 10 \times (12 \sin 8^\circ)$
$\Delta PE \approx 417.5 \text{ J}$ (B1)

(ii) Gain in KE ($\frac{1}{2}mv^2$):
$KE = \frac{1}{2} \times 25 \times 2.4^2$
$\Delta KE = 72 \text{ J}$ (B1)
2. Calculate Work Done against Friction
Work is done to overcome the resistive force. This energy is lost as heat.
$WD_{\text{res}} = \text{Force} \times \text{Distance}$
$WD_{\text{res}} = 50 \times 12$
$WD_{\text{res}} = 600 \text{ J}$ (M1)
3. Apply Work-Energy Principle
The total work done by the pulling force must equal the sum of all energy gains and losses.
$WD_{\text{pull}} = \Delta PE + \Delta KE + WD_{\text{res}}$
$WD_{\text{pull}} = 417.5 + 72 + 600$
$WD_{\text{pull}} = 1089.5$
Total Work $\approx 1090 \text{ J}$ (3 s.f.) (A1)
