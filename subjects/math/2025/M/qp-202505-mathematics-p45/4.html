9709 Math / Mechanics / Q5

A car of mass $1500$ kg is travelling along a straight horizontal road.

(a) It is given that there is a constant resistance to motion. The engine of the car is working at $24$ kW while the car is travelling at a constant speed of $32$ m s$^{-1}$. The power is now increased to $28$ kW. Find the acceleration of the car at the instant it is travelling at a speed of $36$ m s$^{-1}$. [4]

(b) It is given instead that the resistance to the motion of the car is $(340 + 4v)$ N when the speed of the car is $v$ m s$^{-1}$. When the engine is working at $20$ kW, the car is travelling at constant speed. Find this constant speed. [3]

Power (P) 0 kW
Speed (v) 0 m/s
Drive (F) 0 N
Resist (R) 0 N
Speed:
Key Concepts Power: $P = F \times v$ or Driving Force $F = \frac{P}{v}$
Newton's 2nd Law: $F_{net} = ma$
$F_{driving} - F_{resist} = ma$
Step 1: Find Constant Resistance
Initially constant speed ($a=0$) at $32$ m/s with $24$ kW ($24000$ W). At constant speed, Driving Force = Resistance.
$R = F = \frac{P}{v} = \frac{24000}{32}$
$R = 750$ N (B1)
Step 2: Apply N2L at New State
Power increases to $28$ kW ($28000$ W). At the instant $v = 36$ m/s:
New Driving Force: $F_{new} = \frac{28000}{36}$ N
Equation of Motion ($F - R = ma$):
$\frac{28000}{36} - 750 = 1500a$ (M1)

Simplify:
$777.77... - 750 = 1500a$
$27.77... = 1500a$
Step 3: Solve for Acceleration $a = \frac{27.77...}{1500}$
$a \approx 0.0185$ m s$^{-2}$ (or $\frac{1}{54}$) (A1)
Part (b): Variable Resistance
Resistance $R = 340 + 4v$. Power $P = 20$ kW ($20000$ W). Constant speed means $a=0$, so Driving Force = Resistance.
$F = R$
$\frac{P}{v} = 340 + 4v$ (M1)
$\frac{20000}{v} = 340 + 4v$
Solve Quadratic Equation Multiply by $v$:
$20000 = 340v + 4v^2$
$4v^2 + 340v - 20000 = 0$ (DM1)
Divide by 4:
$v^2 + 85v - 5000 = 0$

Factorise or Formula:
$(v - 40)(v + 125) = 0$
Since $v > 0$:
$v = 40$ m s$^{-1}$ (A1)
