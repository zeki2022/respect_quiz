9709 Math / Mechanics / Q3

Summary: Particle starts at $O$ with $v=5$ ms$^{-1}$.
$0-20$s: Accelerates. $20-50$s: Decelerates. At $t=40$, $v=0$.
At $t=T$, particle comes to rest at $O$ ($s=0$).
(a) Find value of $T$.
(b) Find acceleration from $t=50$ to $t=T$.
Time: 0.0 s
Vel: 5.0 m/s
Disp: 0.0 m
Speed:
Step 1: Forward Motion (0-40s)
Velocity at $t=20$:
$v_{20} = u + at = 5 + 0.75(20) = 20$ ms$^{-1}$.

Displacement (Area under graph):
Area 1 (Trapezoid 0-20): $\frac{1}{2}(5+20)\times 20 = 250$ m
Area 2 (Triangle 20-40): $\frac{1}{2}(20)\times 20 = 200$ m
Total Forward $s_{0-40} = 250 + 200 = \mathbf{450}$ m
Step 2: Backward Motion (40s-T)
To return to O, Net Displacement must be 0.
$\therefore$ Backward area must equal $-450$ m.
We know Area(40-50):
Gradient $20 \to 40$ is $\frac{0-20}{20} = -1$.
At $t=50$, $v = 0 + (-1)(10) = -10$ ms$^{-1}$.
Area(40-50) = $\frac{1}{2}(10)(-10) = -50$ m.
Remaining Area needed: $-(450 - 50) = -400$ m.
Step 3: Solve for T (5 marks)
Area from 40 to T is a single triangle.
$\frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height} = 450$
Height is the max negative velocity. But wait, slope is constant from 20s onwards ($a=-1$).
Velocity at T: $v_T = 0 + (-1)(T-40) = -(T-40)$
Area = $\frac{1}{2}(T-40)(-(T-40)) = -450$
$(T-40)^2 = 900 \Rightarrow T-40 = 30$
Wait, gradient changes at $t=50$?
Re-read Q: "decelerates for next 30s" (20 to 50).
So $v_{50} = -10$. From 50 to T is a NEW segment.
Triangle(50-T): Base $(T-50)$, Height $10$.
Area = $\frac{1}{2}(T-50)(10) = 400$ (magnitude)
$5(T-50) = 400 \Rightarrow T-50 = 80$
$T = 130 \text{ s}$
Part (b): Acceleration (2 marks)
From $t=50$ to $t=T(130)$.
Points: $(50, -10)$ and $(130, 0)$.
Gradient = Acceleration
$a = \frac{0 - (-10)}{130 - 50} = \frac{10}{80}$
$a = 0.125 \text{ ms}^{-2}$
