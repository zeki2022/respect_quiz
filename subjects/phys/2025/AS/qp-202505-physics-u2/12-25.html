Question 12
Two resistors connected together have total resistance $R_T = 10\ \Omega$. One is $12\ \Omega$.
What is the resistance of the other?
A. 2.0 Ω
B. 5.5 Ω
C. 17 Ω
D. 60 Ω
Answer: D (60 Ω)

Since $R_T (10) < R_1 (12)$, they must be in Parallel.
$\frac{1}{10} = \frac{1}{12} + \frac{1}{R_2} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{R_2} = \frac{1}{60} \Rightarrow R_2 = 60$.
Question 13
Filament lamp I-V graph. Identical lamp in parallel. Voltage = 6.0 V. Find Total Power.
A. 0.42 W
B. 0.65 W
C. 0.90 W
D. 1.3 W
Answer: D (1.3 W)

From graph at 6.0V, $I \approx 0.11$ A.
Power of one lamp $P = VI = 0.66$ W.
Two lamps in parallel: $P_{total} = 2 \times 0.66 \approx 1.3$ W.
Question 14
Superconducting component S in series with R. Voltage across combo is V.
A. I=V/R, Vs=0
B. I=V/R, Vs=V/2
C. I=inf, Vs=0
D. I=inf, Vs=V/2
Answer: A

Superconductor has $R=0$. So $V_S = IR = 0$.
Total $R_{total} = 0 + R = R$. So $I = V/R$.
Question 15
$R_1 = 14\Omega \pm 20\%$, $R_2 = 30\Omega \pm 10\%$. Series.
A. 13 Ω
B. 9 Ω
C. 7 Ω
D. 6 Ω
Answer: D (6 Ω)

Abs uncertainty adds up.
$\Delta R_1 = 14 \times 0.2 = 2.8$. $\Delta R_2 = 30 \times 0.1 = 3.0$.
Total = $2.8 + 3.0 = 5.8 \approx 6\Omega$.
Question 16
Resistance increased by 15%. Voltage constant. New I and P?
A. 0.87I, 0.87P
B. 0.87I, 1.15P
C. 0.85I, 0.85P
D. 0.85I, 1.15P
Answer: A

$R_{new} = 1.15R$.
$I \propto 1/R \rightarrow 1/1.15 \approx 0.87$.
$P \propto 1/R \rightarrow 0.87$.
Question 17
Mass $M \to 2M$, Spring $k \to 3k$. New frequency?
A. 1.5f
B. 1.2f
C. 0.82f
D. 0.67f
Answer: B (1.2f)

$f \propto \sqrt{k/m}$.
Factor $= \sqrt{3/2} = \sqrt{1.5} \approx 1.22$.
Question 18
Transverse wave $f=2.0$ Hz, $A=3.0$ cm. Distance travelled by a point in 1s?
A. 6.0 cm
B. 8.0 cm
C. 12 cm
D. 24 cm
Answer: D (24 cm)

In 1 cycle, point travels $4 \times A = 12$ cm.
In 1 sec, there are 2 cycles ($f=2$). Total = $2 \times 12 = 24$ cm.

Question 21
Photoelectric. Frequency increases, Power same. Effect on $N$ and $E_{k(max)}$?
A. N dec, Ek inc
B. N dec, Ek same
C. N inc, Ek inc

Answer: A

$E_{photon}$ increases $\rightarrow E_{k}$ increases.

Question 22
Optical fibre time difference formula.
A. (L/v)(1/sin - 1)
B. (L/v)(1 - sin)
C. (L/v)(1/cos - 1)
D. (L/v)(1 - cos)
Answer: C

Path 1: $L$. Path 2: $L/\cos\theta$.
$\Delta t = \frac{L/\cos\theta}{v} - \frac{L}{v} = \frac{L}{v}(\frac{1}{\cos\theta} - 1)$.

Least modal dispersion?

Question 25
Electron diffraction. Momentum decreases. Effect on $\lambda$ and Diameter?
A. Inc, Dec
B. Inc, Inc
C. Dec, Dec
D. Dec, Inc
Answer: B

$\lambda = h/p$. If $p$ decreases, $\lambda$ Increases.
Diffraction spread $\propto \lambda$. So Diameter Increases.
