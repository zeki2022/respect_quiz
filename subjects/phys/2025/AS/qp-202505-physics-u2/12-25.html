Question 12
When two resistors are connected together, the total resistance of the combination is $10\ \Omega$. One resistor has a resistance of $12\ \Omega$.
What is the resistance of the other resistor?
A 2.0 Ω
B 5.5 Ω
C 17 Ω
D 60 Ω
Answer: D

Since $R_{total} (10) < R_1 (12)$, they must be in Parallel.
$\frac{1}{10} = \frac{1}{12} + \frac{1}{R_2} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{R_2} = \frac{1}{10} - \frac{1}{12} = \frac{1}{60}$.
$R_2 = 60\ \Omega$.
Question 13
The graph shows the $I-V$ characteristic of a filament lamp L.
An identical lamp is connected in parallel with L.
The voltage across the parallel combination is $6.0$ V.
What is the total power dissipated by the two lamps?
A 0.42 W
B 0.65 W
C 0.90 W
D 1.3 W
Answer: D

1. From graph, at $V=6.0$V, current $I \approx 110$ mA $= 0.11$ A.
2. Power of one lamp: $P = VI = 6.0 \times 0.11 = 0.66$ W.
3. Total Power (2 lamps in parallel): $P_{total} = 2 \times 0.66 = 1.32$ W.
Question 14
A superconducting component S is placed in series with a resistor of resistance $R$. The voltage across the combination is $V$.
Which row is correct?
A Current in S: $V/R$, Voltage across S: $0$
B Current in S: $V/R$, Voltage across S: $V/2$
C Current in S: infinite, Voltage across S: $0$
D Current in S: infinite, Voltage across S: $V/2$
Answer: A

Superconductor resistance $R_S = 0$.
1. Voltage across S: $V_S = I \times R_S = 0$.
2. Total Resistance = $R$. Current $I = V/R$.
Question 15
Two resistors $R_1$ and $R_2$ are connected in series.
$R_1 = 14\ \Omega \pm 20\%$
$R_2 = 30\ \Omega \pm 10\%$
What is the absolute uncertainty of the total resistance?
A 13 Ω
B 9 Ω
C 7 Ω
D 6 Ω
Answer: D

Absolute uncertainties add up for addition ($R_T = R_1 + R_2$).
$\Delta R_1 = 14 \times 0.2 = 2.8\ \Omega$
$\Delta R_2 = 30 \times 0.1 = 3.0\ \Omega$
Total = $2.8 + 3.0 = 5.8 \approx 6\ \Omega$.
Question 16
A potential difference is applied across a variable resistor.
Current is $I$. Power is $P$.
The resistance is increased by $15\%$. The same potential difference is applied.
Which row shows the new current and power?
A 0.87I, 0.87P
B 0.87I, 1.15P
C 0.85I, 0.85P
D 0.85I, 1.15P
Answer: A

New Resistance $R' = 1.15R$. Voltage $V$ constant.
Current: $I' = \frac{V}{1.15R} = \frac{1}{1.15}I \approx 0.87I$.
Power: $P' = \frac{V^2}{1.15R} = \frac{1}{1.15}P \approx 0.87P$.
Question 17
An object of mass $M$ on a spring of stiffness $k$ oscillates with frequency $f$.
An object of mass $2M$ is suspended from a spring of stiffness $3k$.
What is the frequency of oscillation?
A 1.5f
B 1.2f
C 0.82f
D 0.67f
Answer: B

$f = \frac{1}{2\pi}\sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}$.
$f_{new} = \frac{1}{2\pi}\sqrt{\frac{3k}{2M}} = \sqrt{\frac{3}{2}} \times f$.
$\sqrt{1.5} \approx 1.22 f$.
Question 18
A transverse wave travels along a string.
Frequency $f=2.0$ Hz, wavelength $\lambda=4.0$ cm, amplitude $A=3.0$ cm.
What is the total distance travelled by a point on the string in one second?
A 6.0 cm
B 8.0 cm
C 12 cm
D 24 cm
Answer: D

In one cycle, a point oscillates up and down 4 times the amplitude ($0 \to A \to 0 \to -A \to 0$).
Distance per cycle = $4 \times 3.0 = 12$ cm.
Frequency = 2.0 Hz (2 cycles per second).
Total Distance = $2 \times 12 = 24$ cm.
Question 19
Two aerials $A_1$ and $A_2$ receive radio waves from transmitter T.
Frequency = 104 MHz. Phase difference = 1.4 rad.
What is the minimum value for $d_1 - d_2$?
A 0.32 m
B 0.64 m
C 1.3 m
D 13 m
Answer: B

Wavelength $\lambda = c/f = \frac{3 \times 10^8}{104 \times 10^6} \approx 2.885$ m.
Phase diff $\phi = \frac{2\pi x}{\lambda}$.
$1.4 = \frac{2\pi x}{2.885} \Rightarrow x = \frac{1.4 \times 2.885}{2\pi} \approx 0.64$ m.
Question 20
Young's double-slit apparatus. Fringes with spacing $y$.
Slit separation ($s$) and distance to screen ($D$) are both doubled.
What is the new fringe spacing?
A 4y
B 2y
C y
D y/2
Answer: C

Formula: $y = \frac{\lambda D}{s}$.
New spacing: $y' = \frac{\lambda (2D)}{(2s)} = \frac{\lambda D}{s} = y$. (Unchanged)
Question 21
Ultraviolet light incident on metal. $N$ electrons/sec emitted. Max KE is $E_{k(max)}$.
Frequency increased, Power kept same.
What happens to $N$ and $E_{k(max)}$?
A N decreases, Ek increases
B N decreases, Ek same
C N increases, Ek increases
D N increases, Ek same
Answer: A

1. Photon energy $E = hf$ increases. So $E_{k(max)} = hf - \phi$ Increases.
2. Total Power $P = N_{photons} \times hf$. If $P$ is constant and $hf$ increases, then number of photons ($N_{photons}$) must Decrease. Thus $N$ decreases.
Question 22
Light travels with speed $v$ in optical fibre of length $L$.
Longest path is at angle $\theta$ to axis. Shortest path is along axis. What is the difference between the times taken?
A $\frac{L}{v}(\frac{1}{\sin\theta} - 1)$
B $\frac{L}{v}(1 - \sin\theta)$
C $\frac{L}{v}(\frac{1}{\cos\theta} - 1)$
D $\frac{L}{v}(1 - \cos\theta)$
Answer: C

Shortest distance = $L$. Time $t_1 = L/v$.
Longest distance = $L / \cos\theta$. Time $t_2 = \frac{L}{v\cos\theta}$.
Difference = $\frac{L}{v}(\frac{1}{\cos\theta} - 1)$.
Question 23
Materials refractive indices: Teflon (1.4), Acrylic (1.5), Sapphire (1.7).
Which combination for Core and Cladding results in least modal dispersion?
A Core: Teflon, Cladding: Sapphire
B Core: Sapphire, Cladding: Teflon
C Core: Acrylic, Cladding: Teflon
D Core: Teflon, Cladding: Acrylic
Answer: C

Condition 1: Core index > Cladding index (for TIR). (Eliminates A, D).
Condition 2: Smallest difference in index minimizes modal dispersion (multipath effect).
Sapphire/Teflon diff = 0.3. Acrylic/Teflon diff = 0.1.
So Acrylic core, Teflon cladding is best.

Question 25
Electron diffraction rings. Momentum decreases.
What happens to de Broglie wavelength and Diameter of rings?
A $\lambda$ increases, D decreases
B $\lambda$ increases, D increases
C $\lambda$ decreases, D decreases
D $\lambda$ decreases, D increases
Answer: B

1. De Broglie $\lambda = h/p$. Momentum $p$ decreases $\rightarrow \lambda$ Increases.
2. Diffraction angle $\sin\theta \propto \lambda$. If $\lambda$ increases, angle increases $\rightarrow$ Ring diameter Increases.
