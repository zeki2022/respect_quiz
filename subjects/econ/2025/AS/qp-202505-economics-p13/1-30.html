Question 1
A tourist visits a government-owned tropical beach. She pays $10 to enter but finds it overcrowded. [cite: 501]
Which type of good is the beach? [cite: 504]
A a free good [cite: 506]
B an inferior good [cite: 508]
C a public good [cite: 510]
D a private good [cite: 512]
Answer: D [cite: 841]
Reasoning:
- M1: The beach is excludable because an entry fee of $10 is charged. [cite: 501]
- A1: It is rivalrous because overcrowding means one person's consumption reduces the benefit for others. [cite: 501]
- Conclusion: A good that is both excludable and rivalrous is a private good.
