Question 1
Which statement about a free vaccination programme is normative?
A It will help raise life expectancy.
B It is likely to improve the welfare of society.
C It will result in lower spending on other medical services.
D Tax revenues generated through improved economic activity will exceed the spending on vaccination.
Answer: B

Reasoning: Normative statements express value judgments or opinions about what ought to be. Option B uses the subjective term "improve the welfare" which involves a value judgment. Options A, C, and D are positive statements that can be tested with facts.
Question 2

B Both countries should produce 40 units of capital goods and 20 units of consumer goods.
C Country Y has constant opportunity cost in the production of consumer goods.
D Country Y is producing more consumer goods than country X.
Answer: C

Reasoning: Country Y's PPC is a straight line, indicating constant opportunity cost. Country X's PPC is concave to the origin, showing increasing opportunity cost. Option D cannot be concluded without knowing the actual production point.
Question 3
Which goods are least likely to be allocated by market forces in a mixed economy?
A hospitals
B railways
C street lighting
D television programmes
Answer: C

Reasoning: Street lighting is a public good (non-excludable and non-rivalrous), which tends to be underprovided by the market due to the free-rider problem. Hospitals, railways, and TV programmes can be (and often are) provided by private firms in mixed economies.

Question 5

budget balancecurrent account balance
A $100m deficit$50m deficit

A $100m deficit, $50m deficit
B $100m deficit, $50m surplus

Reasoning: In equilibrium, leakages (S + T + M) = injections (I + G + X). Given S = 100, I = 150, then (I - S) = 50. This 50 must be matched by (G - T) + (X - M). A budget deficit (G > T) of 100 and current account surplus (X > M) of 50 sums to 50, balancing the circular flow.

Question 19
Which statement is not correct?
A The long-run aggregate supply curve can be downward sloping.
B The long-run aggregate supply curve can be horizontal.
C The long-run aggregate supply curve can be upward sloping.
D The long-run aggregate supply curve can be vertical.
Answer: A

Reasoning: In the long run, the aggregate supply curve is typically vertical at the full-employment level of output (classical view) or upward sloping with some flexibility (Keynesian view). A downward-sloping LRAS is not economically plausible as it would imply higher output at lower price levels indefinitely.

Question 22
